### Comprehensive Acknowledgments

- **Cognitive Load Theory**: The application of Cognitive Load Theory (Intrinsic, Extraneous, Germane) to software team boundaries is derived from the seminal work of Matthew Skelton and Manuel Pais in *Team Topologies* (IT Revolution, 2019), which builds on the psychological research of John Sweller (1988). The "Khan Granularity Protocol" utilizes these definitions to calculate architectural scoring.

- **Team API**: The concept and definition of a "Team API" are directly attributed to the Team Topologies framework by Skelton and Pais.

- **Cell-Based Architecture**: The patterns regarding Cell-Based Architecture, "Cell Zero," and partition-based routing are derived from the architectural standards documented in the Amazon Builders' Library, specifically the work of Colm MacCarthaigh on "Shuffle Sharding" and "Static Stability."

- **Forensic Code Analysis**: The methodology for analyzing Git commit history to identify coupling (Recipe 1.1) is adapted from the forensic techniques pioneered by Adam Tornhill in *Your Code as a Crime Scene* (2015).

- **DynamoDB Patterns**: The Single Table Design patterns and "Transactional Outbox" implementations referenced herein are informed by the modeling principles popularized by Rick Houlihan and Alex DeBrie (*The DynamoDB Book*).

- **Shuffle Sharding & Infima**: References to "Infima" and "RubberTree" patterns acknowledge the internal terminology and public engineering disclosures of the Amazon Route 53 engineering team.

- **Platform Engineering**: The "Five Fields" pattern for account vending is adapted from self-service models pioneered by Eventbrite engineering (Terraform Landing Zones).

- **Agentic Patterns**: References to ReAct (Yao et al., 2022) and HyDE (Gao et al., 2022) are cited as academic patterns adapted for enterprise implementation.
